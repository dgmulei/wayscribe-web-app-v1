The October 15 Crisis

Every October, organizations serving Medicare beneficiaries face the same capacity crisis

All 1.8 million Massachusetts Medicare beneficiaries can review coverage during the October 15 to December 7 window. Counselors face predictable surge demand they cannot meet—not because they lack expertise, but because the volunteer model cannot scale to match concentrated AEP timing.

The structural constraint: Recruiting volunteers requires extensive multi-day training sessions and annual recertification. Meanwhile, thousands of beneficiaries are making complex coverage decisions without access to unbiased guidance. And new federal requirements starting in 2027 will transform this annual crisis into year-round compliance requirements.

  • Wrong Medicare Advantage plan = $3,000-7,000 overpaid annually
  • Missing Medicare Savings Program eligibility = $5,000+ in preventable costs
  • Inadequate prescription coverage = thousands in medication costs
  • Working adults locked out = weekday-only appointments when they need help most

    • The time constraint: Counselors spend valuable session time building foundational understanding of each person's situation and teaching beneficiaries what they qualify for and why—covering Medicare Parts A/B/C/D, Medicare Advantage vs Original Medicare, Medigap, Part D drug plans, Medicare Savings Programs, Extra Help eligibility, and MassHealth coordination—before addressing the decisions beneficiaries actually need to make.

    The Capacity Partner Approach

    How Pre-Appointment Education Multiplies Your Existing Capacity

    Beneficiaries complete comprehensive Medicare/MassHealth education before appointments. Counselors assign it when scheduling. Sessions focus on decisions, not explaining Medicare 101.

    Multiply Your Existing Workforce

    Hypothesis: Freeing valuable session time for decision support could significantly increase capacity with existing counselor workforce. If validated statewide, Massachusetts AAAs could serve thousands more beneficiaries during AEP without recruiting volunteers. Sessions shift from education delivery to what counselors do best.

    Beneficiaries Arrive Prepared

    Comprehensive Massachusetts-specific education on Medicare Parts A/B/C/D, Medigap, Medicare Advantage, Medicare Savings Programs (QMB, SLMB, QI), and MassHealth dual-eligible coordination completed before the appointment. Counselors review preparation documents showing what beneficiaries learned and understood.

    24/7 Access for Working Adults

    Working beneficiaries who can't attend weekday appointments access education evenings and weekends. Self-service reduces unnecessary appointment requests for simple questions. More appointment slots available for complex cases requiring counselor expertise during AEP.

    Responsible Stewardship

    At a fraction of volunteer recruitment costs, this represents prudent stewardship compared to training new counselors (substantial investment per volunteer in training time and materials). Pilot validation with measurement ensures impact before organizational commitment. Massachusetts-first approach respects SHINE methodology.

    Language Accessibility

    Beneficiaries learn in their native language—Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole, Chinese, Vietnamese, and 100+ others. Technical Medicare terms explained clearly, documentation generated in their language, family members can help review together. No language barriers to understanding eligibility and options.

    What Beneficiaries Bring to Appointments

    Beneficiaries arrive with personalized documents showing what they've learned—not blank slates requiring full education delivery. Counselors see immediately what ground has been covered.

    Your Medicare Situation Explained

    Sample Document

    What You Qualify For

    QMB (Qualified Medicare Beneficiary Program)

    You qualify because your income is $1,800/month and the limit is $2,935/month in Massachusetts. This means you're earning below 100% of the Federal Poverty Level.

    What QMB Does For You:

    • Pays your Medicare Part B premium ($174.70/month)
    • Covers Medicare deductibles and copayments
    • No cost to you for Medicare-covered services

    Extra Help (Low-Income Subsidy)

    You automatically qualify for Extra Help with prescription drug costs because QMB eligibility means you're under 135% FPL.

    What Extra Help Does For You:

    • Reduces prescription costs to $4.50 per medication
    • No deductible for Part D drug coverage
    • No coverage gap ("donut hole")

    Why These Programs Matter

    Right now, Social Security takes $174.70 from your monthly check for Part B premium. With QMB, that stops. Your diabetes medication costs $300/month. With Extra Help, it becomes $4.50/month.

    Total monthly savings: $470.20

    What Happens Next

    You need to apply for QMB through MassHealth. Once approved, QMB automatically triggers Extra Help for your prescriptions. Your SHINE counselor can help you with the application and explain any questions about coverage.

    Important: The house you sold 2 years ago doesn't affect these programs. The "5-year look back" only applies to nursing home Medicaid, not Medicare Savings Programs.
